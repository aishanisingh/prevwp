The Beginning of a True Love

I've been doing competition math since as long as I can remember. Aged, battered math textbooks are no strange occurence in my house, and it's common to find old pieces of paper with random math computations stuffed in my drawers and scattered all over my room. However, when I recall my past seven or eight year old self, and these math competitions and problems I used to do, I truly cannot remember anything invigorating about them. Sure, they were interesting, and nothing beat the feeling of getting a problem right, but they weren't something that I always had in the back of my mind, or something that I had a real pasion for, for a lack of a better term.
